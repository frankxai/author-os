# Novel Outline Template

> Fill in this template before writing a single chapter. The outline is not a cage — it is a map. You can deviate, but you should know what you are deviating from.

---

## 1. Core Concept

**Premise** (one sentence):
> [A character] must [do something difficult] or [consequence], but [complication].

**Core Dramatic Question:**
> [The single question the entire novel answers. The reader turns pages to find out.]

**Theme** (one word, then one sentence):
> [Word]: [How the novel explores this theme through character action, not lecture.]

**Genre + Comp Titles:**
> [Genre] — like [Comp Title 1] meets [Comp Title 2]

---

## 2. The Arc Cycle

Map your novel's macro-arc:

| Phase | What Happens | Chapters |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Potential** | [The world before. What could be. The crack in the status quo.] | 1-3 |
| **Manifestation** | [Potential becomes real. The inciting incident. No going back.] | 4-6 |
| **Experience** | [Living through consequences. Growth through conflict.] | 7-18 |
| **Dissolution** | [The old form breaks. Crisis. The lowest point.] | 19-22 |
| **Evolved Potential** | [New state. Changed, not restored. New possibility.] | 23-25 |

**Scene purpose key:**
- **P** = Plot advancement
- **C** = Character development
- **W** = World-building
- **T** = Theme exploration
- **R** = Relationship development

Every scene should serve at least two purposes. Scenes that serve only one should be examined for merger or cut.
